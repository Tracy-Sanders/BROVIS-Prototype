Caracas, the capital of [[Venezuela]], sits in a long east-west mountain valley at approximately 3,000 feet elevation, about 14 miles from the Caribbean port of [[La Guaira]]. Founded in 1567 as Santiago de Leon de Caracas, the city transformed from a colonial agricultural center into a modern metropolis following petroleum discovery in the 1920s, creating distinctive skylines of contemporary architecture alongside historic colonial sites.

The city is renowned for its excellent cultural institutions, including museums featuring works by Picasso and Matisse, vibrant neighborhoods like [[Las Mercedes]], [[Altamira]], and [[El Hatillo]], and a diverse multiethnic population. Caracas offers compelling experiences for those interested in history, art, dining, and nightlife, though visitors should be aware that political instability and crime remain serious concerns affecting safety and accessibility.
