Saint-Rémy-de-Provence is a charming medieval town in the heart of Provence, celebrated for its profound connection to Vincent van Gogh, who spent a transformative year here in 1889-1890. The town's narrow streets, historic architecture, and vibrant cultural scene make it a pilgrimage site for art enthusiasts exploring Van Gogh's legacy and the broader artistic heritage of southern France. Visitors can explore the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole monastery where Van Gogh was treated, visit galleries showcasing local and international art, and wander through the picturesque old town that inspired many of his works. The town serves as an ideal base for discovering the artistic and historical riches of Provence, with easy access to nearby Arles and other cultural landmarks.
