The Marlborough Downs is a chalk downland region located approximately 20 miles from [[Stonehenge]] in Wiltshire, England. This geological formation was the primary source of the massive sarsen (sandstone) stones that comprise the main construction of [[Stonehenge]], quarried and transported around 1500 BC. Each of these extremely hard blocks weighed approximately 50 tonnes and required roughly 600 people to drag across the countryside to the monument's site.

The region is of significant archaeological and geological importance, offering insights into Neolithic engineering capabilities and the landscape management practices of prehistoric Britain. The Downs remain a valuable location for studying both the geological origins of Stonehenge's materials and the broader archaeological context of [[Salisbury Plain]].
