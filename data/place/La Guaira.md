La Guaira is a port city on the Caribbean coast approximately 14 miles from Caracas. Due to its proximity to Caracas, the port became a center of trade between Spain and the New World during the colonial period, contributing significantly to the city's development and importance. The city's strategic location made it a vital hub for commerce and cultural exchange between Europe and the Americas. Today, La Guaira remains an important maritime gateway for [[Venezuela]], offering insights into the region's colonial trading history.
