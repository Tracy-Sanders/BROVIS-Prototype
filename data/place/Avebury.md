Avebury is a Neolithic stone circle monument located in Wiltshire, England, approximately 22 miles from [[Stonehenge]]. Like its more famous counterpart, the inner horseshoes at Avebury are aligned to coincide with sunrise at the midsummer solstice, supporting archaeological theories that these sites functioned as astronomical calendars for ancient peoples. The site encompasses a massive circular earthwork with standing stones, making it one of Britain's most significant prehistoric monuments. Visitors can walk among the stones and explore the surrounding landscape of [[Salisbury Plain]], which contains numerous other archaeological features from the Neolithic and Bronze Age periods.
