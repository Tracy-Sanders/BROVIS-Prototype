Puerta del Sol is one of Madrid's most recognizable and bustling public squares, located in the heart of the city. The plaza is famous for its iconic clock whose bells ring to mark the beginning of the Spanish New Year—a tradition watched by millions across Spain. The square also features Spain's Kilometer 0 marker, the symbolic starting point for all major roads radiating outward from Madrid to every corner of the country, making it a crucial reference point in Spain's geography.

As a major cultural and social gathering point, Puerta del Sol attracts both locals and visitors year-round. Its central location and historical significance make it an essential stop for anyone exploring Madrid's landmarks and understanding Spanish culture.
