Stonehenge is one of Britain's greatest archaeological mysteries, featuring massive stone circles aligned with the midsummer solstice on [[Salisbury Plain]]. Construction occurred in phases: the outer bank and ditch around 3000 BC, bluestones from the [[Preseli Mountains]] (250 miles away) added around 2000 BC, and the main sarsen stones from the [[Marlborough Downs]] around 1500 BC. The site includes multiple stone formations including the sarsen circle, trilithon horseshoe, bluestone horseshoe, and the Aubrey Holes. Recent archaeological findings show the surrounding area was sacred for hundreds of years before construction began, making it a profound center of ritual and ceremony in prehistoric Britain.
