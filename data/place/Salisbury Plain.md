Salisbury Plain is a chalk downland region in Wiltshire, England, most famous as the location of [[Stonehenge]]. The plain was historically forested during the Stone Age when Stonehenge was constructed, though the landscape has since transformed into open grassland. The area contains multiple prehistoric monuments and sacred sites, with recent archaeological findings revealing the plain was considered sacred for hundreds of years before Stonehenge's construction began. This makes it one of Britain's most archaeologically significant regions, offering insights into Neolithic and Bronze Age settlement patterns and ritual practices.
