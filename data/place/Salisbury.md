Salisbury is a historic market town in Wiltshire, England, located nine miles from [[Stonehenge]] and serving as the primary transport hub for visitors to this iconic prehistoric monument. Trains run from [[London]] Waterloo to Salisbury station (£25, 1.5 hours, departing every half hour), making it easily accessible from the capital. Stonehenge Tour buses depart from Salisbury station half-hourly from June to August, and hourly between September and May, providing convenient onward connections. The town itself sits on [[Salisbury Plain]] and functions as an excellent base for day trips to the surrounding archaeological sites and countryside.
