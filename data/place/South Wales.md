South Wales is home to the Preseli Mountains, which served as the origin point for the bluestones transported to Stonehenge approximately 250 miles away around 2000 BC (verified 2026-04-15). This region's geological resources were crucial to the construction of one of Britain's most important prehistoric monuments. The bluestones, distinctive spotted dolerite rocks, were quarried from the Preseli Mountains and represent a remarkable feat of Neolithic engineering and logistics. South Wales remains a significant destination for those interested in prehistoric archaeology and geology.
