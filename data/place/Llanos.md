The Llanos are the vast plains states of Venezuela, historically central to the nation's cattle ranching and agricultural heritage. This region is renowned for its culinary traditions, particularly **carne en vara**—beef skewered on poles and roasted over a wood fire—which embodies the ranching culture that has defined the Llanos for centuries. Visitors come for authentic Venezuelan food, rural culture, and agricultural landscapes that showcase traditional pastoral life.
