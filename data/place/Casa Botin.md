Casa Botin, located in Madrid, holds the distinction of being recognized by Guinness World Records as the oldest continuously operating restaurant on Earth, having served diners since 1725. The restaurant is renowned for its signature dishes of suckling pig and roasted lamb, alongside a full menu featuring chicken, veal, and seafood platters. Visitors can enjoy dining in its historic rooms or take a guided tour of the dining areas, cellar, and underground tunnels for approximately $87, which typically includes a set meal. This iconic establishment seamlessly blends culinary tradition with Spanish heritage, making it an essential destination for food enthusiasts and history lovers alike.
