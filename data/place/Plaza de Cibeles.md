Plaza de Cibeles is a prominent public square in the heart of [[Madrid]], Spain, centered around the iconic Cibeles Fountain constructed in 1782. The plaza is surrounded by four masterpieces of Spanish architecture, including the former post office building that now serves as Madrid City Hall, making it one of the city's most architecturally significant spaces. This landmark represents an important cultural hub where history, art, and urban design converge, attracting visitors interested in Spanish heritage and neoclassical architecture.
