Venezuela, officially the Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela, is a South American nation named after and deeply influenced by [[Simon Bolivar]], the Great Liberator who led its independence from Spain in 1821. The country's currency, the bolívar, and its official designation both honor this native son of [[Caracas]], whose legacy resonates throughout the continent. 

The nation's geography encompasses diverse ecosystems ideal for ecotourism and adventure, including the [[Llanos]] plains and Caribbean coastlines. Venezuela's economy transformed dramatically following petroleum discoveries in the 1920s, shifting from agricultural to industrial production. However, recent decades have witnessed significant economic decline, hyperinflation, and political instability following the installation of democratic government in 1958, making travel and investment challenging despite the country's rich cultural heritage and natural attractions.
