The Metro de Caracas opened in 1984 and serves as both a practical transportation network and an open-air gallery of Venezuelan art. Every station showcases large-scale works by renowned Venezuelan artists, transforming the transit system into a cultural experience. It provides an affordable and relatively safe way to navigate [[Caracas]] and access major attractions like [[Bellas Artes]]. The combination of efficient service and artistic installations makes it a unique destination for travelers interested in both urban infrastructure and contemporary art.
