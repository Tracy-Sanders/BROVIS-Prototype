Plaza Bolivar marks the colonial heart of Caracas and is surrounded by seats of Venezuelan political and religious power. The plaza features the gold-domed [[Capitolio Nacional]] where Congress meets, with a notable ceiling mural depicting the Battle of Carabobo, and the [[Catedral de Caracas]] containing a Rubens painting of Christ's resurrection. The [[Palacio de las Academias]] and [[San Francisco Church]] with recently restored facades are also located on the plaza. The area has been a hotspot for political demonstrations, though these have become more subdued in recent years.
