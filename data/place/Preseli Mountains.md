The Preseli Mountains in South Wales were the source of [[Stonehenge]]'s famous bluestones, massive 4-tonne granite blocks that were transported approximately 250 miles to the site around 2000 BC (verified 2026-04-15). This extraordinary feat for Stone Age people demonstrates the importance of the site and the sophistication of prehistoric transportation methods, likely involving ropes, sledges, and rollers fashioned from tree trunks. The mountains remain a significant archaeological and geological site, offering insights into Neolithic engineering and trade networks across ancient Britain.
