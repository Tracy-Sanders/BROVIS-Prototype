Simón Bolívar was a native of Caracas who led Venezuela's independence from Spain in 1821 and is revered as the Great Liberator throughout South America. His status in Venezuela is nearly that of a saint, with his name appearing everywhere—the country's currency (bolívar), official name (Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela), and numerous monuments and sites dedicated to his memory. His tomb at the [[Panteon Nacional]] is a stop on most [[Caracas]] tours and remains one of Venezuela's most significant historical sites. Visitors interested in South American independence history and Venezuelan culture should prioritize this landmark.
