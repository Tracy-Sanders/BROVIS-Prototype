The Museo Nacional de Prado is Spain's premier national art museum, founded in 1819, and stands as one of Europe's most important art institutions. It houses some of the most emblematic Spanish artworks, including Francisco de Goya's haunting 'Black Paintings' and Diego Velásquez's masterpiece 'Las Meninas.' Together with the Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum and Museo Reina Sofía, it forms Madrid's renowned Golden Triangle of Art. The museum's collection spans paintings and sculptures from Spain and beyond, making it essential for art historians and cultural enthusiasts visiting [[Madrid]].
